Methodology

How our calculations work

This page explains the logic behind our transport emission calculator in a transparent and simplified way. We combine distance-based inputs with emission factors from publicly available reports, then estimate the total CO₂ impact per trip. The objective is to make each result easy to understand, compare, and challenge.

The detailed methodology is available in our report, while this page highlights the formulas and baseline values used in the app itself.

Core formulas

How We Calculate And Estimate Emissions:

1. General emissions model

For most transport modes: \[ CO_2 = d \cdot ef \]

  • \(CO_2\): total emissions for a trip (g CO₂)
  • \(d\): trip distance (km)
  • \(ef\): emission factor per passenger-km (g CO₂/pkm)

2. Car with shared passengers

For private cars we adjust per passenger: \[ CO_2 = \frac{d \cdot ef}{p} \]

  • \(p\): number of passengers in the vehicle
  • Higher occupancy lowers emissions per person

3. Electric vehicle approximation

For electric vehicles we estimate emissions from electricity use: \[ ef = ec \cdot x \]

  • \(ec\): electricity consumption (kWh/km)
  • \(x\): grid intensity (g CO₂/kWh)

4. Monthly footprint estimate

Summed over multiple trips in a period: \[ CO_{2,month} = \sum_{i=1}^{n} d_i \cdot ef_i \]

  • \(n\): number of trips in the selected period
  • Each trip can have its own transport mode and factor

Reference values used in the app

The following values are representative baseline factors used for comparison in the calculator. View the paper for more details on how these were derived and the assumptions behind them.

5. Air travel

  • Economy class: 127 g CO₂/pkm
  • Premium class: 155 g CO₂/pkm
  • Business class: 284 g CO₂/pkm

6. Rail travel

  • Diesel train: 91 g CO₂/pkm
  • Electric rail (Nordic mix): 10 g CO₂/pkm

7. Urban public transport

  • Light rail (Bybanen-like): 4 g CO₂/pkm
  • Diesel bus: 30 g CO₂/pkm
  • Biodiesel bus: 14 g CO₂/pkm
  • Electric bus: 13 g CO₂/pkm

8. Individual mobility and ferry

  • E-scooter (operational estimate): 0–29 g CO₂/pkm
  • Passenger ferry (conventional): 186 g CO₂/pkm
  • Private car: depends on size, fuel, and occupancy
